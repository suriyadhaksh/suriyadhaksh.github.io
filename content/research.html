Modeling & Theory

Asymptotic modeling of electrochemical transport from micro- to nano-scales

We develop perturbation-based linear approximations for Poisson–Nernst–Planck systems to understand boundary-layer behavior in ion transport and electric fields. These models cleanly separate diffusive, slow outer dynamics from electromigrative, fast boundary-layer processes, enabling computationally efficient reduced-order descriptions.

Coupled diffuse-interface and multiphysics models with guaranteed energy dissipation

Our work focuses on developing a quasi-incompressible Navier–Stokes–Cahn–Hilliard system coupled with Poisson–Nernst–Planck equations that ensures mass conservation and strict energy dissipation. We use this continuum framework to study electrophoretic motion of cells and electromagnetic casting.

Inverse Cumulative Distribution Function (iCDF) formulation for population balance equations

To address the bounded particle-size domain in standard population balance equations, we employ an inverse-CDF–based transformation that tracks the cumulative distribution instead of particle sizes directly. This approach converts the PBE into a stable transport problem while preserving total droplet mass in evolving distributions.

Numerical Methods

Linearized and semi-implicit finite element schemes for coupled Navier–Stokes–Poisson–Nernst–Planck

We design finite element schemes for tightly coupled multiphysics PDEs. My work focuses on linearization and semi-implicit time stepping that remain robust at strong coupling strengths, allowing larger time steps and reducing nonlinear solver costs.

Conservative finite element schemes for three-phase Cahn–Hilliard

We develop mass-conserving numerical methods for ternary Cahn–Hilliard systems, ensuring accurate interface dynamics and morphology evolution in multicomponent materials.

Software

Scalable multiphysics solvers on octree-based geometries

I build MPI-parallel, PETSc-based finite element solvers running on adaptively refined octree meshes. These tools target large-scale simulations involving fluid flow, electrochemistry, and multicomponent phase fields on complex geometries, enabling efficient parallel performance on modern HPC systems.
