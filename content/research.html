Modeling & Theory

Asymptotic modeling of electrochemical transport

We develop perturbation-based linear approximations for Poisson–Nernst–Planck systems to understand the extremely thin boundary-layer behavior of ion transport and electric fields near domain boundaries. These models separate slow, diffusive, outer dynamics from fast, electromigrative boundary-layer processes, enabling computationally efficient reduced-order descriptions.

Coupled diffuse-interface and multiphysics models

Our work focuses on developing a quasi-incompressible Navier–Stokes–Cahn–Hilliard system coupled with Poisson–Nernst–Planck equations that ensures mass conservation and strict energy dissipation. Using this continuum framework, we aim to study electrophoretic motion of cells and phase evolution in electromagnetic casting.

Inverse Cumulative Distribution Formulation (iCDF) for population balance equations

To address the bounded particle-size domain in standard population balance equations, we employ an inverse-CDF–based transformation that tracks the cumulative distribution instead of particle sizes distribution directly. This approach converts the PBE into a stable transport problem while preserving total droplet mass in evolving distributions.

Numerical Methods

Fully linearized schemes for Navier–Stokes–Poisson–Nernst–Planck equations

Our work focuses on developing semi-implicit finite element schemes for NS-PNP system to simulate electrohydrodynamic phenomena. We aim to achieve fully linearized updates with large time steps, reducing nonlinear solver costs and enabling GPU acceleration to simulate stiffer flow regimes.

Conservative finite element schemes for three-phase Cahn–Hilliard system

We design mass-conserving and energy-stable numerical methods for ternary Cahn–Hilliard systems to generate diverse material morphological data sets for various systems' chemistry.

Software

Scalable multiphysics solvers on complex geometries

We build MPI-parallel, PETSc-based finite element solvers running on adaptively refined octree meshes. These tools target large-scale simulations involving fluid flow, electrochemistry, heat transfer and multicomponent phase fields on complex geometries, enabling efficient parallel performance on modern HPC systems.
