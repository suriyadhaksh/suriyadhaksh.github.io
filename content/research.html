Modeling & Theory

Asymptotic modeling for electrochemical transport phenomena at micro-scales

I develop perturbation-based and multiscale approximations for Poisson–Nernst–Planck systems to understand ion transport, potential structure, and boundary-layer behavior. These models separate slow outer dynamics from fast interfacial processes, producing interpretable and efficient reduced-order descriptions.

Coupled diffuse-interface and multiphysical models ensuring energy dissipativeness

My work includes building thermodynamically consistent formulations—particularly Navier–Stokes–Cahn–Hilliard models—where flow, concentration, and interfaces evolve together while maintaining an energy-dissipation structure. This enables stable long-time simulations of phase separation and interfacial transport.

Population balance equation modeling using cumulative distribution function representation

I explore CDF-based transformations of population balance equations to track particle size distributions without introducing numerical diffusion. This approach converts the PBE into a stable transport problem that preserves sharp features in evolving distributions.

Numerical Methods

Linearized and semi-implicit finite element schemes for coupled Navier–Stokes–Poisson–Nernst–Planck

I design stable finite element strategies for tightly coupled multiphysics PDEs. My work focuses on linearization and semi-implicit time stepping that remain robust at strong coupling strengths, allowing larger time steps and reducing nonlinear solver costs.

Conservative finite element schemes for three-phase Cahn–Hilliard

I develop mass-conserving numerical methods for ternary Cahn–Hilliard systems, ensuring accurate interface dynamics and morphology evolution in multicomponent materials.

Software

Scalable multiphysics solvers on octree-based geometries

I build MPI-parallel, PETSc-based finite element solvers running on adaptively refined octree meshes. These tools target large-scale simulations involving fluid flow, electrochemistry, and multicomponent phase fields on complex geometries, enabling efficient parallel performance on modern HPC systems.
